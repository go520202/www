Sunday morning, Hyeon-hui got on a bus going to Saemgol, her father's hometown.

Through the car window, she saw beautiful rural scenery.

"Hyeon-hui, when I was a child, I played happily on the riverside. For example, catching fish."

"The fishes live now, don't they?"

Hyeon-hui was glad to think that she could see fish that enjoyed swimming in the river.

She thought the bus was too slow.

They got off the bus and left for Saemgol.

Flowers on roadside seemed to greet her smilingly.

When they got to the riverside,

they smelled something.

It was from the stream.

"Oh! Since when has it become like this?"

Father was surprised.

"Daddy, do the fish live?"

"The river is polluted, so the fishes have moved to another place."

Father's face was gloomy while looking at the river.

They didn't see clear water or swimming fishes.

Hyeon-hui felt sorry.

After coming back home, Hyeon-hui wrote in her diary about what happened during the day.
