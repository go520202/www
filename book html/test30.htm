Once upon a time, there was a county magistrate who dealt with things as he wished in his town.

One winter day, he wanted to eat wild berries.

So, he called a petty official.

"Petty official, I want to have wild berries, so, go to the mountain and get them for me."

The petty official answered that there were no wild berries because it was winter.

Thereupon, the county magistrate told him that he would punish him if he did not get the wild berries.

The Cowardly petty official was sick in his bed.

He had a smart son.

His son asked why he was sick in bed.

"Son, I don't know what I can do? The county magistrate told me to get wild berries."

"Father, it is winter now. Where can we find the wild berries?"

"However, I'm afraid the county magistrate will punish me if I don't get the wild berries. I'll be in big trouble."

"Father, don't worry. I will go to him and come back."

His son visited the county magistrate and said that his father was sick.

"Well, your father must be pretending to be ill."

"No. when my father went to get the wild berries, he was bitten by a snake."

The county magistrate was speechless with amazement.

"You, young rascal! How could there be a snake in winter"

The petty official's son said politely,

"Yes, as there is no snake in winter, there are also no wild berries.

Please let my father recover."

The county magistrate was sorry for what he had done.

The petty official recovered because of his son's wisdom, and was not punished.
